# Log 001 — Ian Finlay's Synthetic Proof

## Introduction

The concurrency of the three medians is one of the classical theorems of Euclidean geometry. Since antiquity, it has been proved using a wide variety of methods, including synthetic constructions, coordinate geometry, vector methods, affine geometry, and barycentric coordinates.

The purpose of the present project is not to establish a new proof of the theorem. Instead, the project investigates how a classical synthetic proof can be reconstructed, analyzed, documented, and ultimately prepared for formal verification in Lean.

In 2015, Ian Finlay presented a remarkably concise synthetic proof on Mathematics Stack Exchange. This proof serves as the initial case study of the CGJteam Lab project.

## Theorem

**Centroid Theorem.**

The three medians of every non-degenerate triangle are concurrent. Furthermore, their common intersection point (the centroid $G$) divides each median in the ratio 2:1, with the longer segment adjacent to the corresponding vertex.

## Ian Finlay's Construction

Let $AD$ and $BE$ be two medians of triangle ABC, intersecting at point $G$.

1. Extend the segment $AG$ beyond $G$ to a point $P$ such that $\vert AG \vert = \vert GP \vert$.
2. Using the Midsegment Theorem in triangles $\triangle ABP$ and $\triangle ACP$, it follows that $CG \parallel BP$ and $BG \parallel CP$.
3. Consequently, quadrilateral BGCP is a parallelogram.
4. The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect one another.
5. The line through vertex $A$ and point $G$ passes through the midpoint of side $BC$.
