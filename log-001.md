# 📝 Log-001 — Formalizing Finlay's Synthetic Proof

**Date:** 2026-07-11  
**Project:** CGJteam Lab  
**Status:** First Milestone

---

## 1. Beginning

The initial objective of the project was deliberately modest. Rather than attempting to formalize Euclidean geometry as a whole, the goal was to verify a single elegant result: Ian Finlay's synthetic proof that the three medians of a triangle are concurrent.

At first sight, this appears to be a self-contained exercise in formal verification. During the development, however, it gradually became clear that the proof itself suggests the structure of the library required to support it. The geometry library, therefore, was not designed *a priori*—it emerged from the proof.

---

## 2. Finlay's Argument

Let △ABC be a triangle. Let E and F be the midpoints of AC and AB, respectively, and let **G = BE ∩ CF**. To prove that G is the centroid of △ABC, Finlay introduces an auxiliary point P such that G is the midpoint of AP.



The argument proceeds through the following steps:

1. **Równoległość (część I):** Ponieważ F i G są środkami boków AB i AP, otrzymujemy:
   `FG ∥ BP`
   Z faktu, że C, F i G są współliniowe, wynika, że:
   `CG ∥ BP`

2. **Symmetryczna równoległość:** Analogicznie, ponieważ E i G są środkami boków AC i AP, mamy:
   `EG ∥ CP`
   Co implikuje:
   `BG ∥ CP`

3. **Wniosek o równoległoboku:** Z powyższych zależności wynika bezpośrednio, że czworokąt **BPCG jest równoległobokiem**.

4. **Zakończenie:** Niech `D = AP ∩ BC`. Ponieważ przekątne równoległoboku przecinają się w połowie, D jest środkiem boku BC. Oznacza to, że AD jest medianą △ABC. Ponieważ G leży na odcinku AP, punkt G należy również do trzeciej mediany. Zatem wszystkie trzy mediany △ABC przecinają się w jednym punkcie.

---

## 3. Formalization Strategy

The objective of the Lean development was not merely to reproduce the final conclusion. Instead, every mathematical step of Finlay's proof was formalized independently. 

The resulting proof mirrors the original argument almost line by line. Rather than becoming a monolithic Lean script, the proof became a structured composition of reusable geometric theorems.

---

## 4. From Proof to Library

An unexpected consequence of the formalization was that several intermediate arguments naturally became independent library results:

- `MidsegmentParallel`
- `ParallelogramParallelRecognition`
- `ParallelogramDiagonalMidpoint`
- `MidpointSymmetry`

Although introduced solely to support Finlay's proof, these theorems immediately became reusable components for future developments. Thus, the geometry library was not designed *a priori*; it emerged as a mathematical by-product of the proof itself.

---

## 5. Current Status

The first milestone of the project has been reached. The complete logical structure of Finlay's proof has been translated into Lean while preserving the sequence of the original geometric argument. 

Only after achieving this faithful formalization does it become meaningful to ask a second question: which assumptions are genuinely primitive, and which can themselves be proved? That investigation marks the beginning of the next stage of the project.
