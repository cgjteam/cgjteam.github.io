# Log 001 — The Concurrency of Triangle Medians

**Method:** Synthetic Geometry

### Introduction
The medians of a triangle are classical objects in Euclidean geometry. This series analyzes the synthetic construction proposed by Ian Finlay for the concurrency of the medians and investigates its logical dependencies.

### Theorem
The three medians of a triangle are concurrent. Furthermore, the centroid divides each median in the ratio $2:1$, with the longer segment adjacent to the vertex. This property holds for every non-degenerate triangle.

### Dependencies
* **Definitions:** Midpoint, Median
* **Theorems:** Midsegment Theorem
* **Lemma:** The 2:1 Ratio on Two Medians

(Note: This lemma serves as a foundational proof tool for the subsequent analysis of Finlay's construction.)

### Proof of the 2:1 Ratio
Let $AD$ and $BE$ be two medians of triangle $ABC$. Let $G = AD \cap BE$. (The existence and uniqueness of $G$ rely on the fact that distinct medians of a triangle are not parallel. While vector methods confirm this fact's independence from the concurrency theorem, within the scope of the present analysis, no independent synthetic proof has been identified.)

1. Construct segment $DE$ connecting the midpoints of $AC$ and $BC$.
2. By the Midsegment Theorem: $DE \parallel AB$ and $|DE| = \frac{1}{2} |AB|$.
3. Since $D, G, A$ and $E, G, B$ are collinear, and $DE \parallel AB$, we have:
$$\angle GDE = \angle GAB, \qquad \angle GED = \angle GBA$$



4. By the AA Similarity Criterion, $\triangle GDE \sim \triangle GAB$.
5. Since $\frac{|DE|}{|AB|} = \frac{1}{2}$, the similarity ratio equals $\frac{1}{2}$.
6. It follows that $|AG| = 2|GD|$ and $|BG| = 2|GE|$, implying $|AG| : |GD| = |BG| : |GE| = 2 : 1$.

Therefore, point $G$ divides medians $AD$ and $BE$ in the ratio $2:1$.

### Sources
* Primary source: Ian Finlay, *Proving that the medians of a triangle are concurrent*, Mathematics Stack Exchange, 2015 (synthetic construction adapted and analyzed).
