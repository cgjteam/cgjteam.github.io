# Encapsulating Synthetic Arguments

**How recurring geometric arguments became reusable components of the geometry library.**

**Date:** 2026-07-13  
**Project:** CGJteam Lab  
**Status:** Library Development

---

## 1. Overview

The formal verification of Finlay's proof produced considerably more than a single verified theorem.

During the implementation, several geometric arguments appeared repeatedly in different parts of the proof. Rather than reconstructing these arguments each time, they were gradually extracted as independent theorems of the developing geometry library.

This log records the first stage of that process.

---

## 2. The First Encapsulated Argument

The first complete synthetic argument extracted from the proof was the Midsegment Theorem.

Instead of reproducing its construction whenever it was needed, the entire proof was encapsulated as a reusable theorem.

```lean
have hFGparallelBP :=
  MidsegmentParallel
    B P A F G
    hFB hGP
```

### Mathematical meaning

`MidsegmentParallel` states that the segment joining the midpoints of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side.

### Library role

The theorem encapsulates the complete synthetic proof of the Midsegment Theorem, allowing later developments to reuse the argument without reconstructing it.

---

## 3. Helper Lemmas

Several elementary geometric transformations were promoted to independent helper lemmas.

| Theorem | Mathematical meaning |
|---------|----------------------|
| `MidpointSymmetry` | If M is the midpoint of AB, then M is also the midpoint of BA. |
| `ParallelSymmetry` | Exchanges the two parallel lines. |
| `ParallelSwapFirstLine` | Reverses the orientation of the first parallel line. |
| `ParallelSwapSecondLine` | Reverses the orientation of the second parallel line. |
| `ParallelSwapBoth` | Reverses the orientation of both parallel lines. |
| `collinear_parallel_substitute` | Replaces one endpoint of a parallel segment by a collinear point. |
| `collinear_parallel_substitute2` | Symmetric version of the previous construction. |

These lemmas describe elementary transformations that repeatedly occur in synthetic geometry. Their introduction significantly reduced repetitive proof steps.

---

## 4. Accessor Lemmas

The project also introduced a first collection of accessor lemmas.

Instead of repeatedly extracting information from compound geometric definitions, the required properties became available through dedicated theorems.

| Theorem | Extracted information |
|---------|-----------------------|
| `MedianIntersectionCollinearCGF` | Points C, G and F are collinear. |
| `MedianIntersectionCollinearBGE` | Points B, G and E are collinear. |
| `SegmentIntersectionCollinearADP` | Points A, D and P are collinear. |
| `SegmentIntersectionCollinearBDC` | Points B, D and C are collinear. |

These lemmas introduce no new geometry. Their purpose is to expose frequently used geometric facts through descriptive mathematical names.

---

## 5. First Naming Decisions

The introduction of reusable components also influenced the naming policy.

Names were no longer chosen according to the proof in which a theorem first appeared.

Instead, they began to describe the underlying mathematical object.

Examples include

- `MidsegmentParallel`
- `MidpointSymmetry`
- `ParallelSymmetry`

This convention makes the library progressively independent of the individual proofs from which its components originally emerged.

---

## 6. Outlook

The encapsulation process described in this log represents the first step from formalizing individual proofs towards constructing a reusable geometry library.

The next stage will focus on organizing these components into coherent mathematical theories, beginning with the theory of parallelograms.
