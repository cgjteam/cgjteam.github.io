# Log-019
# Tarski as a Third Route to Finlay's Proof

**Date:** 2026-07-23
**Project:** CGJteam Lab
**Status:** Independent Axiomatic Development

---

## 1. Overview

The geometry library now contains a third route to the Midsegment Theorem
and Finlay's proof. This route begins with Tarski's primitive language of
betweenness and segment congruence.

The purpose of the development is not to replace the stable Hilbert-based
path or the alternative Suppes path. It provides another controlled
axiomatic branch whose higher-level conclusions are expressed through the
common language of `GeometryBase`.

The resulting architecture is:

```text
FinlayProofTarski
└─ MidsegmentParallelTarski
   └─ TarskiBase
      ├─ GeometryBase
      │  └─ HilbertAxioms
      │     └─ GeometryCore
      └─ TarskiAxioms
         └─ GeometryCore
```

`GeometryCore` remains the deepest shared language. No second point type
and no duplicate betweenness or congruence relations are introduced.

---

## 2. The Primitive Tarski Language

The module

```lean
TarskiAxioms.lean
```

reuses three objects already provided by `GeometryCore`:

- `Geo.Point`,
- `Geo.Between`,
- `Geo.Congruent`.

The Tarski axioms are organized into three layers:

```text
TarskiNeutral
└─ TarskiPlane
   └─ TarskiEuclideanPlane
```

`TarskiNeutral` contains the dimension-independent neutral axioms:
congruence reversal, congruence transitivity, congruence identity,
segment construction, the five-segment axiom, betweenness identity and
inner Pasch.

`TarskiPlane` extends the neutral layer with the lower and upper
two-dimensional axioms.

`TarskiEuclideanPlane` adds the first form of Euclid's axiom, with the
conclusion containing the betweenness relation

```lean
Geo.Between X T Y
```

The continuity schema is intentionally deferred. `DecidableEq` is not
treated as a geometric axiom.

---

## 3. Mathematical and Licensing Boundaries

The formalization is mathematically based on Alfred Tarski and Steven
Givant's presentation of Tarski geometry.

GeoCoq was consulted only as a check on the completeness and hierarchy of
the axiom system. Its code and proofs were not copied or translated. This
separation is required because CGJteam Lab is MIT-licensed, whereas GeoCoq
is distributed under LGPL-3.0.

The Tarski development is therefore an independent Lean formalization of
the mathematical system, not a port of another proof library.

---

## 4. TarskiBase as an Integration Layer

The module

```lean
TarskiBase.lean
```

introduces two notions expressed entirely in Tarski's primitive language:

```lean
TarskiCollinear
TarskiIsMidpoint
```

Three points are Tarski-collinear when one of the three cyclic
betweenness relations holds. A Tarski midpoint combines betweenness with
congruence of the two component segments.

`TarskiBase` also provides basic cyclic collinearity results and the
conversion of a Tarski midpoint into the shared `IsMidpoint` notion.

The central architectural object is:

```lean
TarskiGeometryBaseBridge
```

This bridge is explicit because the incidence relation used through
`GeometryBase` and the `Geo.OnLine` relation from `GeometryCore` are
distinct formal objects. The bridge records the compatibility between
Tarski collinearity and the incidence-based `Collinear` relation.

It is important not to overstate this construction. The bridge does not
claim that the current Tarski axioms derive the complete
`HilbertIncidence` interface. It isolates the compatibility assumption
needed by the present comparison of proof routes.

## 5. The Tarski Midsegment Route

The module

```lean
MidsegmentParallelTarski.lean
```

imports only `TarskiBase`. Its public theorem,
`MidsegmentTheoremTarski`, receives two midpoint assumptions expressed as
`TarskiIsMidpoint`.

The proof proceeds in two stages.

First, the explicit bridge converts the Tarski midpoint data into the
shared midpoint language. Second, the geometric construction is carried
out through the reusable operations of `GeometryBase`: segment
extension, triangle congruence, parallelism, parallelogram recognition
and transfer along a collinear line.

This organization separates two responsibilities:

```text
TarskiBase
    interprets primitive Tarski data

MidsegmentParallelTarski
    performs the shared geometric construction
```

The module does not import the stable `MidsegmentParallel.lean`. The two
routes remain independent at the level of theorem modules even though
they use the same lower-level geometric vocabulary.

---

## 6. Finlay's Proof in Tarski Form

The module

```lean
FinlayProofTarski.lean
```

imports only `MidsegmentParallelTarski`.

Its theorem `FinlayTarski` states the midpoint and collinearity
assumptions in Tarski's language:

- the three midpoint hypotheses use `TarskiIsMidpoint`,
- the four incidence hypotheses use `TarskiCollinear`.

The proof invokes `MidsegmentTheoremTarski` twice. The bridge then
translates only the collinearity facts needed by the common
parallelogram and intersection arguments.

The conclusion combines:

- `IsMedian` from the shared geometry language,
- `TarskiCollinear` for the final collinearity of the centroid line.

This makes the interface boundary visible in the theorem statement
rather than hiding all translations inside implementation details.

## 7. Three Controlled Proof Routes

The library now supports three paths to the same geometric development:

```text
Hilbert route:
FinlayProof
└─ MidsegmentParallel

Suppes route:
FinlayProofSuppes
└─ MidsegmentParallelSuppes

Tarski route:
FinlayProofTarski
└─ MidsegmentParallelTarski
```

The Hilbert route remains the stable reference implementation. The
Suppes route studies a constructive affine presentation based on
midpoint and doubling operations. The Tarski route studies a point-only
axiomatic language based on betweenness and congruence.

The comparison is possible because all three routes eventually meet the
shared vocabulary exposed by `GeometryBase`.

---

## 8. File Structure and Current Status

The Tarski branch deliberately uses four modules:

```text
TarskiAxioms.lean
TarskiBase.lean
MidsegmentParallelTarski.lean
FinlayProofTarski.lean
```

No `TarskiCore.lean` or `TarskiTheorems.lean` layer is introduced. At the
current scale, those additional files would separate closely related
definitions and elementary results without clarifying the dependency
structure.

All four modules compile successfully with Lean 4.33. The development
adds no continuity schema and makes no claim that the explicit bridge has
already been derived from the Tarski axiom hierarchy.

---

## 9. Next Step

The next architectural question is whether progressively larger parts of
the shared incidence and geometric interface can be derived from the
Tarski hierarchy rather than supplied through
`TarskiGeometryBaseBridge`.

Any such refinement should preserve the current public route:

```text
FinlayProofTarski
→ MidsegmentParallelTarski
→ TarskiBase
```

This allows the implementation beneath the bridge to evolve without
changing the higher-level mathematical narrative.
