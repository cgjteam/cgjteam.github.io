# Log 018: Why a Single Geometry.lean File?

## Introduction
Once the architecture of the project had been established, a practical design decision remained: how should the formal implementation itself be organized? One possibility was to distribute the development across many Lean files, each corresponding to a theorem or research log. Another was to maintain a single, continuously evolving formal library. CGJteam Lab adopts the second approach.

## Two Possible Strategies
There are two natural ways to organize a formal mathematics project:
1. **Modular implementation** — many small Lean files, each containing a separate portion of the development.
2. **Centralized implementation** — one principal library containing all formal objects.

Both approaches are technically valid. The choice depends on the objectives of the project.

## Why a Single Library?
The goal of CGJteam Lab is not simply to verify individual theorems. The objective is to construct a coherent synthetic geometry library whose components naturally interact. Definitions become prerequisites for axioms, axioms support derived lemmas, and derived lemmas become building blocks for increasingly sophisticated theorems. Maintaining these objects within a single library emphasizes their logical relationships rather than their historical order of development.

## The Separation of Responsibilities
The project distinguishes three independent layers:
* **Research Logs** explain mathematical ideas and document the research process.
* **Geometry Library Index (`Geometry.md`)** records the architecture of the library, identifiers, dependencies, and implementation metadata.
* **Geometry.lean** contains the complete formal implementation.

Each layer has a distinct purpose and evolves independently.

## Advantages of a Single Source File
The centralized approach offers several benefits:
* Every formal object is located within one coherent mathematical environment.
* Refactoring becomes simpler because declarations remain in a common context.
* Dependencies are easier to analyze.
* The implementation reflects the logical organization of synthetic geometry rather than the chronology of the blog.

As the project expands, the single library becomes a continuously evolving mathematical knowledge base rather than a collection of unrelated proofs.

## Research Perspective
The blog documents the evolution of ideas. `Geometry.md` documents the evolution of the architecture. `Geometry.lean` documents the evolution of the formal implementation. These three views remain synchronized while serving different audiences: mathematicians, developers, and the Lean proof assistant.
