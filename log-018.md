# Log-018
# Refactoring the Hilbert and Suppes Architecture

**Date:** 2026-07-23
**Project:** CGJteam Lab
**Status:** Axiomatic Architecture Refactoring

---

## 1. Overview

The project architecture has been reorganized to clarify how the
Hilbert-based and Suppes-based developments descend toward their
axiomatic foundations.

The purpose of this change is not to separate the two developments
completely. `GeometryBase` remains the common geometric language used
by both versions of Finlay's proof. The Suppes route adds an alternative
affine justification of the Midsegment Theorem while preserving the
shared vocabulary of the existing library.

`FinlayProof.lean` remains the stable reference development.
Architectural experiments continue in `FinlayProofSuppes.lean` until
the two midsegment interfaces can be unified.

---

## 2. Descending Through the Hilbert Route

The direct import of `GeometryCore` was removed from
`GeometryBase.lean`. It was redundant because `HilbertAxioms.lean`
already imports `GeometryCore.lean`.

The resulting dependency route is:

```text
FinlayProof
└─ MidsegmentParallel
   └─ GeometryBase
      └─ HilbertAxioms
         └─ GeometryCore
```

This diagram should be read from the concrete proof downward toward its
foundations. The role of `HilbertAxioms` is not merely to store axioms
removed from `GeometryBase`. In the intended development, the geometric
results currently postulated in `GeometryBase` should eventually be
proved from a complete formalization of Hilbert's axioms.

The simplified import chain was checked by compiling
`FinlayProof.lean` successfully.

---

## 3. Reducing the Suppes File Structure

The earlier Suppes development used three successive modules:

```text
SuppesAxioms
→ SuppesCore
→ SuppesTheorems
```

At the current scale of the project, this division introduced more
layers than were useful. `SuppesCore` contained only the primitive
triangle and parallelogram definitions, while `SuppesTheorems`
contained the results derived from the Suppes axioms.

The contents of these two modules have therefore been consolidated
into:

```lean
SuppesBase.lean
```

The former `SuppesCore.lean` and `SuppesTheorems.lean` files were
removed.

---

## 4. The Role of SuppesBase

`SuppesBase` imports:

```lean
CGJteamLab.GeometryBase
CGJteamLab.SuppesAxioms
```

It contains:

- the primitive Suppes triangle and parallelogram definitions,
- the theorems formerly contained in `SuppesTheorems`,
- `SuppesParallel`,
- `suppes_parallel_of_parallelogram`,
- `SuppesMidsegmentBridge`.

`SuppesBase` is not a replacement for `GeometryBase`. It is an
integration layer connecting the affine language of Suppes with the
common language already used in the geometry library.

This distinction is important because the axioms of Suppes are not
being claimed to be equivalent to Hilbert's axioms. The present Suppes
formalization is based on midpoint, doubling and collinearity, whereas
`GeometryBase` also exposes notions such as congruence, angles and
parallelism.

The bridge states explicitly how the particular Suppes constructions
needed for the Midsegment Theorem are interpreted through the common
geometric interface.

---

## 5. The New Suppes Dependency Route

`MidsegmentParallelSuppes.lean` now imports only:

```lean
import CGJteamLab.SuppesBase
```

The file can therefore concentrate on the Suppes-based proof of the
Midsegment Theorem itself.

The resulting architecture is:

```text
FinlayProofSuppes
└─ MidsegmentParallelSuppes
   └─ SuppesBase
      ├─ GeometryBase
      │  └─ HilbertAxioms
      │     └─ GeometryCore
      └─ SuppesAxioms
         └─ GeometryCore
```

`GeometryBase` remains present in both proof routes. The Suppes branch
adds an alternative route for the midsegment step without replacing the
shared terms used by the rest of Finlay's proof.

---

## 6. Linter and Compilation Checks

The theorem `suppes_parallel_of_parallelogram` does not use the
section-level `HilbertIncidence` instance. This was recorded explicitly:

```lean
omit [HilbertIncidence Geo] in
theorem suppes_parallel_of_parallelogram
```

This removes the unused-section-variable warning without disabling the
linter and without changing the assumptions required later by
`SuppesMidsegmentBridge`.

After the refactoring, both developments compile successfully:

```text
FinlayProof.lean
FinlayProofSuppes.lean
```

---

## 7. Next Step

The two versions of Finlay's proof now form a controlled environment for
interface unification. Work will continue in `FinlayProofSuppes.lean`
while `FinlayProof.lean` remains the reference version.

The next objective is to reduce the difference between
`MidsegmentTheorem` and `MidsegmentTheoremSuppes` until the chosen
realization can be selected primarily by changing the imported
midsegment module.
