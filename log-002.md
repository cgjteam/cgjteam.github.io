# Log-002 — The First Steps in Lean

**Date:** 2026-07-12  
**Project:** CGJteam Lab  
**Status:** Entering Formal Geometry

---

## 1. Overview

The completion of Finlay's mathematical argument marked only the beginning of the project.

The next challenge was to express the proof in Lean.

At first this appeared to be a straightforward translation. It soon became evident that formalization requires considerably more than rewriting mathematical prose. Every implicit geometric argument must become an explicit theorem, every construction must be justified, and every dependency must be stated precisely.

The first encounter with Lean therefore became an exploration of the structure hidden inside an apparently simple synthetic proof.

---

## 2. The First Obstacle

Finlay's proof relies repeatedly on the Midsegment Theorem.

For a human reader, the statement

> "the segment joining two midpoints is parallel to the third side"

is immediate.

For Lean, however, this theorem did not exist.

Consequently, the first major objective became the formal proof of the Midsegment Theorem itself.

Rather than serving merely as an auxiliary lemma, it became the first substantial theorem proved inside the synthetic geometry framework.
