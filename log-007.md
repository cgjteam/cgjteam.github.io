# Log 007 — The Diagonals of a Parallelogram

### Introduction
The final step of Ian Finlay's construction relies on a single classical property of parallelograms. Once the quadrilateral $BGCP$ has been established as a parallelogram, the remainder of the proof follows from the behavior of its diagonals. This log isolates that property and explains its role in the overall argument.

### Theorem
The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other. Equivalently, if the diagonals intersect at a point $M$, then:
$$BM = MC \quad \text{and} \quad GM = MP$$
Thus, the intersection point is the midpoint of both diagonals.



### Application to Finlay's Construction
In the previous log, the quadrilateral $BGCP$ was shown to be a parallelogram. Its diagonals are $BC$ and $GP$. Let $M = BC \cap GP$. Since the diagonals of a parallelogram bisect one another, the point $M$ is the midpoint of segment $BC$.

Because $M$ also lies on the diagonal $GP$, and $A, G,$ and $P$ are collinear by construction, it follows that $A, G, M$ are collinear. Therefore, the line through $A$ and $G$ passes through the midpoint of side $BC$. Hence, $AG$ is the third median of $\triangle ABC$.

### Logical Role
This theorem completes the transition from the auxiliary construction to the original geometric statement. The midpoint of $BC$ is not introduced directly; instead, it is recovered through the geometry of the parallelogram. This is the final logical bridge in Finlay's proof.
