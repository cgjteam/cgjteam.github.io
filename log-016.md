# Log 016: Why Geometry.md Exists

## Introduction
As the formal geometry library grows, the number of definitions, axioms, lemmas, and theorems increases rapidly. While the Lean source code provides the formal implementation, it is not designed to serve as architectural documentation. Conversely, a mathematical blog explains ideas but does not capture implementation details. This observation motivates the introduction of a third component of the project: **Geometry Library Index (`Geometry.md`)**.

## Three Complementary Views
The CGJteam Lab project is organized around three complementary representations of the same mathematical development.

1. **Research Logs** explain the mathematics. They present ideas, proofs, motivations, and methodological discussions in a form intended for human readers.
2. **Geometry.md** documents the formal architecture. It records every formal object, its identifier, dependencies, implementation status, and its relationship to other components of the library.
3. **Geometry.lean** contains the formal implementation accepted by the Lean proof assistant.

These three representations describe the same mathematical development from different perspectives.

## Why Not Document Everything Inside Lean?
Lean source files are optimized for formal verification rather than navigation. As the project evolves, declarations may be reordered, proofs optimized, and internal implementations modified. Such changes are natural during development but make the source code unsuitable as the primary documentation layer. The architectural information should therefore remain independent of implementation details.

## Why Not Document Everything in the Blog?
Research logs are narrative documents. They explain mathematical ideas rather than catalogue formal objects. Repeating dependency graphs, implementation status, and metadata in every article would make the blog difficult to maintain and would duplicate information already available elsewhere. Instead, each log concludes with a brief reference to the formalization, while the complete architectural information is maintained centrally in `Geometry.md`.

## The Role of Geometry.md
`Geometry.md` functions as the central index of the project. For every formal object it records:
* a unique identifier,
* the corresponding Lean declaration,
* its mathematical role,
* logical dependencies,
* related research logs,
* implementation status,
* documentation status.

This makes the document the bridge between the mathematical narrative and the Lean implementation.

## Research Perspective
The separation of narrative, documentation, and implementation reflects the philosophy of the project. Mathematics is communicated through research logs, organized through the architectural index, and verified through Lean. None of these representations replaces the others; together they form a coherent formalization workflow.
