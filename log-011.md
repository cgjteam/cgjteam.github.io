# Log 011 — Primitive Predicates Become Definitions

## Summary

This log documents the refactoring of the Geometry library after completing the formal proof of Finlay's theorem.

The objective was to distinguish genuinely primitive notions from concepts that can be introduced definitionally. The complete proof continued to compile after these changes.

## Refactoring Summary

The following primitive predicates were replaced by ordinary definitions:

- `IsMidpoint`
- `IsMedian`
- `IsParallelogram`

The following axioms became ordinary theorems:

- `midpoint_collinear`
- `midpoint_congruent`
- `MidpointMedian`
- `ParallelogramOfParallel`

The complete formalization of Finlay's theorem continued to compile after these modifications.

---

## Observation

The reverse engineering process showed that several predicates originally implemented as primitive notions were, in fact, definitional abbreviations built from more elementary relations.

The refactoring reduced the number of primitive concepts without requiring any modification of the overall proof strategy.
