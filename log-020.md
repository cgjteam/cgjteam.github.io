# Log 020: From Research Project to Formal Geometry Library

## Introduction
The first stage of the CGJteam Lab project began with a single question: can Ian Finlay's elegant synthetic proof of the concurrency of the medians be reconstructed, analyzed, and prepared for formal verification? Twenty research logs later, the project has evolved into something broader. What began as the study of one classical proof has become the design of a reusable formal geometry library together with a methodology for documenting, organizing, and verifying mathematical knowledge.

## What Has Been Established?
The first phase of the project has produced four complementary results:
1. A complete logical reconstruction of Finlay's proof.
2. A dependency graph describing the proof architecture.
3. A formal documentation system based on stable mathematical identifiers.
4. An implementation strategy centered around a single evolving Lean library.

Together, these components form the foundation upon which future formal developments can be built.

## Three Layers of the Project
The project is now organized into three complementary layers:
* **Research Layer:** The Research Logs explain mathematical ideas, document investigations, and record methodological decisions.
* **Documentation Layer:** The **Geometry Library Index (`Geometry.md`)** provides the permanent architectural description of the formal library. It records identifiers, dependencies, implementation status, and relationships between formal objects.
* **Implementation Layer:** **`Geometry.lean`** contains the executable formal mathematics accepted by the Lean proof assistant.

These layers are independent but synchronized, allowing each to evolve without compromising the others.

## Beyond Finlay's Proof
The concurrency of the medians was never intended to be the final objective. Instead, it serves as the initial case study through which the methodology of CGJteam Lab has been developed. The same workflow can now be applied to other synthetic proofs, allowing their logical architectures to be reconstructed, documented, and formally verified within the same geometry library.

## Looking Ahead
Future work will focus on expanding both the mathematical content and the formal library:
* New definitions, lemmas, and theorems will be integrated into `Geometry.lean`.
* `Geometry.md` will continue to serve as the architectural map of the library.
* The Research Logs will document not only successful formalizations but also alternative proof architectures, optimization strategies, dependency analyses, and methodological questions arising during development.

## Research Perspective
The long-term objective of CGJteam Lab is not merely to verify isolated theorems. It is to develop a reusable, well-documented, and logically organized library of synthetic geometry in which mathematical ideas, formal documentation, and executable proofs evolve together.
